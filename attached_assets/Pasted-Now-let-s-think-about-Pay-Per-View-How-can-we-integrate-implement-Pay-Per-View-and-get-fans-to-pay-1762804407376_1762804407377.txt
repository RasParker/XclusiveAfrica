Question 3: Access Duration
Recommendation: Permanent Access

Data costs matter: In many African markets, data is expensive. If users pay for content and then lose access after 30 days, they feel pressured to download/watch immediately, which creates friction. Permanent access means they can revisit content when they have WiFi or cheaper data plans.

Trust building: Permanent access builds trust. New platforms need to prove value, and "pay once, keep forever" is a clearer, more trustworthy proposition than time-limited access.

Social sharing: When users know they have permanent access, they're more likely to recommend content to friends, creating organic marketing.

Why this dual model works:
Economic flexibility: Not everyone can commit to recurring monthly subscriptions. PPV allows casual fans to engage with specific premium content they really want without the commitment.

Conversion funnel: PPV acts as a "trial" - fans who pay for 2-3 videos might realize a subscription is better value and convert. You're not leaving money on the table from users who would never subscribe but will pay for specific content.

Price optimization:

Make PPV slightly more expensive per piece (e.g., $2-3 per video)
